TCS Round (new!)

Problem Statement

You have an $n \times n$ square grid of lightbulbs, where $n = 10^{10}$. Initially, each lightbulb is either on or off, chosen at random. On any move, you may select 3 lightbulbs which form an isosceles right triangle of minimal size on the grid (i.e. an L-shaped tromino), and flip the state of all 3 lightbulbs (so if they were on before, they will now be off, and vice versa). Find an algorithm that, in at most k1019 moves total, will allow you to turn off every lightbulb in the grid.
