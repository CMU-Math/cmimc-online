Math Contest Format

The contest consists of five rounds: TCS round, team round, and three individual rounds.

TCS Round (new!)

The main idea behind this round is algorithmic design. We will give you a problem that is either an optimization problem where you try to get a close approximation to optimal or a deterministic problem.

You may find a few different solutions to the problems, but they must be deterministic problems.

We will give you points based off of which complexity bounds your solutions beat.

You can think of the complexity bounds as saying "have no more than $N$ steps in your algorithm" or "try to maximize your score, $N$, in the game"

More technically, the idea is to design and prove the correctness of an algorithm. If you have an $O(N \log N)$ solution, you will get more points than an $O(N^2)$ solution according to our rubric.

By prove, we mean that you must show that your algorithm is correct and terminates.

Team Round

The team round will be 60 minutes long, with fifteen short-answer problems. The problems will be taken from a variety of subjects.

Individual Round

The individual rounds each consists often short-answer problems, to be solved in 60 minutes. Each competitor takes all three individual rounds: Algebra and Number Theory, Combinatorics and Computer Science, and Geometry.
