CMWMC Info

About

This year, we are also hosting Carnegie Mellon’s Womens Math Competition (CMWMC). This contest is designed for girls who are interested in mathematics, regardless of their previous experience or current skill level. Our mission is to ultimately create an encouraging environment for girls in STEM. During the contest, there will be both individual and team rounds based on Number Theory, Algebra, and Computer Science in addition to mini events and our faculty guest speaker.

The contest will take place in October 29th, 2022 and will be in-person at Carnegie Mellon. Registration will open soon.

Format

Format is subject to change.

Individual Round: There will be one individual round, containing problems on Algebra, Number Theory, Geometry, Combinatorics, and Computer Science.

Relay Round: Teams of 3 will work to solve sets of problems where each team member works on one problem of the set and passes their answer to the next team member, who will use that answer to solve their own problem (like a relay!). No other communication is allowed. You don’t have to wait for the person before you to finish before you start working on your problem. Only the last contestant’s submission will be graded.

Guts Round: Teams of 3 will work together to solve sets of problems. After completing one set, the next problem set will be given. Teams cannot return to edit answers from a previous set.
